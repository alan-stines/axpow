Who We Are

The forerunner of AXPOW was first conceived in 1942 by two mothers from New Mexico whose sons had been taken prisoner following the Battle of Bataan.

AXPOW eventually grew into a Congressionally chartered non-profit veterans' service organization dedicated to assisting former POWs and civilian internees and their families. Our work includes documenting the service of those held captive, maintaining historical records, and advocating for survivors' benefits.

As time has passed, preserving the history of American POWs and educating the public about the sacrifices they made only grows in importance.
