Before Texas and Oklahoma were synonymous with modern oil, there was Ploiesti. During World War II, the Romanian refineries supplied a major share of Axis fuel, making them one of the most important strategic targets in Europe.

On August 1, 1943, Allied forces launched **Operation Tidal Wave**, a low-level bombing raid involving 178 B-24 Liberators. The mission required extreme range, strict radio silence, and precise navigation over hostile territory.

The raid was marked by weather complications, navigation errors, and fierce anti-aircraft resistance. Aircrews flew at dangerously low altitude through smoke, fire, and concentrated ground defenses.

The cost was severe, and many aircraft did not return. Although the mission damaged refinery capacity, German forces restored significant production sooner than expected.

Even so, the raid remains one of the most dramatic and courageous aerial operations of the war and a lasting testament to the sacrifice of Allied airmen.
